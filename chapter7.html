Chapter 7 Bankruptcy

Do you need a make a fresh start?

Are you unable to pay off your credit card debt? Are you facing large medical bills as the result of a recent hospitalization? Are you caught in a cycle of taking out payday loans?

A Chapter 7 bankruptcy could help.

What is Chapter 7?

Chapter 7 bankruptcy allows you to have a fresh start -- meaning that upon the discharge of your Chapter 7, bankruptcy most debts are cancelled. (Typical exceptions include tax obligations, child support, and student loans, unless the court rules otherwise.)

Types of debts that are dischargeable in a Chapter 7 bankruptcy include: credit cards, medical bills, payday loans, signature loans, and past due utility bills. You can also typically maintain secured debt, such as your home and your car, as long as you remain current on your obligations to those creditors.

An added bonus of a Chapter 7 bankruptcy is that it STOPS collections action, including garnishment, bank levy, and pending court hearings.
